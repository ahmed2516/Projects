FAQS

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What programs does the University of stirling offer?▼
A: The University of stirling offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various disciplines including business, engineering, health, humanities, and sciences.
Q: How do I apply for admission?▼
A: You can apply for admission online through our website. Please visit the admissions section for detailed instructions and application deadlines.
Q: Can international students apply?▼
A: Yes, international students are welcome to apply. Please refer to the international admissions section on our website for specific requirements and application procedures for international applicants.
Q: What are the campus facilities like?▼
A: The University of stirling provides modern campus facilities including libraries, laboratories, sports centers, and student accommodation. You can explore our campus virtually or schedule a campus tour to see them firsthand.
Q: Can I work part-time while studying?▼
A: Yes, many students work part-time jobs while studying to support themselves financially. The University of stirling offers various opportunities for part-time employment both on-campus and in the local community. However, it's important to balance work with your studies to ensure academic success.
Q: What are the tuition fees?▼
A: Tuition fees vary depending on the program of study and your residency status. Please refer to the tuition fees section on our website for current rates.
Q: Are scholarships available?▼
A: Yes, the University of stirling offers scholarships and financial aid to eligible students. Please visit the scholarships and bursaries section on our website for more information.
Q: How can I contact student services?▼
A: You can contact student services by phone, email, or in person. Visit the student services section on our website for contact information and office hours.
Q: What support services are available for students?▼
A: The University of stirling offers a range of support services to help students succeed academically, emotionally, and socially. This includes academic advising, counseling services, disability support, career guidance, and more. Our dedicated student support team is here to assist you throughout your academic journey.
Q: Are there opportunities for research or internships?▼
A: Yes, the University of stirling encourages students to engage in research projects, internships, and work placements to gain practical experience and enhance their skills. We have partnerships with industry leaders and research institutions to provide valuable opportunities for students to apply their learning in real-world settings.
